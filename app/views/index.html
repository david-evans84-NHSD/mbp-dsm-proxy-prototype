Greeting

If you start your message with a greeting make sure you use "Dear" followed by the patient's name and not "Hi" or similar.

Choosing which name to use depends on the context of the message. Use a first name when you want to be more encouraging or conversational. For a more formal or reassuring tone consider using a full name.

Example
  • conversational tone – "Dear ((firstName))"
  • reassuring tone – "Dear ((firstName)) ((lastName))"

Avoid using just a last name or first name without a greeting as it can sound impersonal.

For proxies, address the patient and not the proxy (parent or carer) to avoid confusion over who the communication is intended for. For new borns or children you may want to address the parent or carer. If you do you should include the name or date of birth of the patient in the body of the message to avoid confusion.

Example
  • "Dear parent or carer, we are inviting your child born in ((month))((year)) for free NHS vaccinations to keep them healthy."
  • "Dear parent or carer of ((firstName)). This is a reminder of your appointment which is booked for ((day)) ((month)) at ((time)) at ((location))."
