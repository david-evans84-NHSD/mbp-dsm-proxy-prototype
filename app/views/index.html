Greeting

If you start your message with a greeting make sure you use "Dear" followed by the patient's name and not "Hi" or similar.

Choosing which name to use depends on the context of the message. Use a first name when you want to be more encouraging or conversational. For a more formal or reassuring tone consider using a full name.

Example
  • conversational tone – "Dear ((firstName))"
  • reassuring tone – "Dear ((firstName)) ((lastName))"

Avoid using just a last name or first name without a greeting as it can sound impersonal.

Parent, carer and proxy

If you are writing to someone who has another person looking after their health (sometimes called a proxy), greet the patient and not their carer to avoid confusion over who the communication is intended for.

Try to avoid using the word "proxy". Research has shown users prefer "carer" or "trusted person".

However, for newborns or children greet the parent or carer. Try to include an identifying detail such as the name or date of birth of the child in the body of the message.

Example
  • for children - "Dear parent or carer, we are inviting your child born in ((month))((year)) for free NHS vaccinations to keep them healthy."

You can apply this guidance for greeting proxies across all channels, including text messages, emails and paper letters.
